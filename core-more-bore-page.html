Structuring Content for Impactful eLearning

Overview

We adopt the Core / More / Bore model as a strategic approach to designing engaging, effective, and scalable digital learning experiences. This model supports differentiated learning by prioritising the most essential content for all learners (Core), providing optional deep-dives (More), and removing or reframing less critical or overly detailed content (Bore).

What is Core / More / Bore?

Core
The essential content every learner must understand and apply. Drives the key learning outcomes and aligns with compliance, performance, and behavioural objectives.

More
Enrichment or optional detail for those who want to go deeper. Encourages learner agency, supports self-directed learning, and builds capability for advanced users.

Bore
Excess or low-impact detail that may confuse or disengage learners. Reviewed critically and either removed, summarised, or converted into downloadable/reference content.

Why It Matters for ISQ

ISQ supports educators and leaders across a diverse range of schools, each with different levels of familiarity and readiness. The Core / More / Bore model allows us to: Respect learners’ time by focusing on the highest-impact knowledge. Design for different contexts by offering optional content. Support PD compliance by clearly marking must-complete elements. Enhance user experience with intuitive, decluttered course flows. Ensure scalability and reusability of our content assets.

How It’s Applied in Practice

Storyline
Core content is placed in the primary slide layer.
More content appears as popups, tabs, buttons, or links (e.g. "Want to learn more?").
Bore content is distilled into downloadable PDFs, optional links, or removed during SME reviews.

Rise
Core blocks lead each section (e.g. Text, Image, Video).
More is added using Accordion, Knowledge Check, or Button blocks to external links.
Bore is trimmed or offloaded to downloadable resources or Tooltips.

H5P
Core learning objectives are directly assessed through Quizzes or Interactives.
More content appears in expandable panels or branching scenarios.
Bore is removed entirely or placed in expandable content behind icons or “info” markers.
